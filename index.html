New York Rising Community Reconstruction Program Community Mapping

Welcome to the New York Rising Community Reconstruction Program community mapping pages. These maps will help communities provide input into a Community Reconstruction Plan being developed through the New York Rising Community Reconstruction Program. Driven by a local Planning Committee and public input, the Plan will support recovery and increase resiliency for communities affected by Hurricanes Sandy and Irene and Tropical Storm Lee.

An initial Conceptual Plan outlining the community’s assets and needs will be shortly with a final Community Reconstruction Plan to be released in March 2014.

This tool will allow members of the following communities to provide input on their Community Reconstruction Plans:

  • Breezy Point
  • New and Old Howard Beach
  • Lower Manhattan
  • Red Hook
  • Rockaway East
  • Rockaway West

What information are you requesting from me?

Community Asset Information

Community asset information, which includes a variety of places and resources within a community:

  • Economic (ex: shopping corridors, visitor destinations)
  • Housing (ex: vulnerable residential neighborhood)
  • Infrastructure (ex: subway line, electrical substation)
  • Natural and Recreational Resources (ex: public parks, beaches)
  • Health and Social Services (ex: hospitals, community centers)

What to do: Tell us what we have missed or gotten wrong by clicking on an asset and entering a comment under "Corrections."

Storm Recovery and Resiliency Needs

Storm recovery and resiliency needs may be specific to certain assets (ex: a community facility that needs a generator) or community-wide (ex: emergency response systems). Needs may address:

  • Repairing or replacing damaged assets
  • Making existing assets more resilient
  • Creating new assets critical to resiliency

What to do: Click on an asset or the community-wide map and give us input under the "Needs" tab.

Ideas for rebuilding and resiliency

Ideas for rebuilding and resiliency can range from providing a backup generator for a facility, to new coastal protections, to developing a new emergency care facility. They may be specific to certain assets, or community-wide.

What to do: Click on an asset or the community-wide map and give us input under the "Ideas" tab.

What will you do with this information?

Your input will be visible to all other map users, your community Planning Committee, its planning consultants, and the NY State New York Rising team. We will work to compile your input and incorporated into our draft and final Community Reconstruction Plan:

  • All corrected community asset information will be incorporated into the Plan’s asset inventory
  • We will compile the storm recovery and resiliency needs to identify common threads and issues, which will help us to prioritize the needs to be addressed
  • We will compile your ideas for projects and actions for rebuilding and resiliency and assess their feasibility, costs, and benefits in order to draft a priority list in the Plan
