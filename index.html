New York Rising Community Reconstruction Program Community Mapping

What information are you requesting from me? What will you do with this information?

Welcome to the New York Rising Community Reconstruction Program community mapping pages. These maps will help residents provide input into their Community’s Reconstruction Plan.

The Community Reconstruction Plans will support recovery and increase resiliency for communities affected by Hurricanes Sandy and Irene and Tropical Storm Lee. The first phase of the New York Rising Community Reconstruction Program requires two key plan submissions:

  • An initial DRAFT Conceptual Plan outlining the community’s assets and needs – due Oct 28th.
  • A Final Community Reconstruction Plan proposing strategies and projects to meet the community’s short and long term goals - due March 31st 2014.

What information are you requesting from me?
  1. Confirm important Community Assets
  2. Tell us Storm Recovery and Resiliency Needs
  3. Provide Ideas for Rebuilding and Resiliency
Instructions

Click on places or resources within your community.

A box will appear where you can tell us what we missed, what’s important to you, or any big ideas you may have – click on each tab to enter your feedback

Assets include a variety of places and resources within a community, categorized by:

  • Economic (shopping corridors, visitor destinations)
  • Housing (vulnerable residential neighborhood)
  • Infrastructure (subway line, electrical substation)
  • Natural and Recreational Resources (public parks, beaches)
  • Health and Social Services (hospitals, community centers)
What will you do with this information?

We will compile your input into the draft and final Community Reconstruction Plan:

  • All corrected community asset information will be incorporated into the Plan’s asset inventory
  • We will compile the storm recovery and resiliency needs to identify common threads and issues, which will help us to prioritize the needs to be addressed
  • We will compile your ideas for projects and actions for rebuilding and resiliency and assess their feasibility, costs, and benefits in order to draft a priority list in the Plan

Your input will be visible to all other map users, your community Planning Committee, the Community planning consultants, and the NY State New York Rising team.
