New York Rising Community Reconstruction Program Community Mapping

What information are you requesting from me? What will you do with this information?

Welcome to the New York Rising Community Reconstruction Program community mapping pages. For the latest updates on the planning process, please click here.


These maps were published in October 2013 to help residents provide input into their Community’s Reconstruction Plan, which will support recovery and increase resiliency for communities affected by Hurricanes Sandy and Irene, and Tropical Storm Lee. As of February 7th 2014, these maps have been closed for comments as the NY Rising process nears completion.

Preliminary Conceptual Plans were released in November 2013. A Final Community Reconstruction Plan proposing strategies and projects to meet the community’s short and long term resiliency goals will be completed and released by April 2014.

Access maps for each NY Rising Community planning area:

  • Breezy Point
  • Howard Beach
  • Lower Manhattan
  • Red Hook
  • Rockaway East
  • Rockaway West
How will these maps be used?

Over the course of 4 months, 310 comments were collected. This public input will help to guide the Planning Committee, its consultants and the NY State Office of Storm Recovery in developing projects and actions for rebuilding and resiliency to be included in the Final Community Reconstruction Plans.

What if I have more input to give?

Your voice and your input are important, and we are continuing to have community discussions. Please direct any comments about Breezy Point, Howard Beach, Lower Manhattan, Red Hook, Rockaway East or Rockaway West to: nyrising@hraadvisors.com (include your community’s name in the subject line).

If you have more general comments or questions about the NY Rising program as a whole, please email the NY State Office of Storm Recovery at info@stormrecovery.ny.gov
